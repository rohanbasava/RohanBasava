About Me

I think in systems,
build in prototypes.

I am a sophomore at Purdue University in the Integrated Business and Engineering program, a selective dual-degree track that trains students to operate across both the business and technical sides of an organization. I am specializing in Financial Engineering with a Minor in Finance.

Growing up, I was always the person who wanted to understand why something worked, then figure out how to make it work better. That curiosity led me from building robotics competition hardware to researching mathematical structures to writing a novel. Not because I could not commit to one thing, but because I genuinely believe the most interesting problems live at the intersection of disciplines.

Right now I am a Marketing and Operations Intern at SeedlingLabs, an AI-native startup where I run campaigns, build dashboards, and help commercialize products that bridge the digital divide across India. It is the kind of work where technical literacy and business strategy are equally important, which is exactly where I want to be.

I am looking for internship opportunities in product strategy, AI go-to-market, and venture, roles where I can keep learning at the pace I have set for myself and contribute meaningfully from day one.

toward.

AI That Actually Reaches People
Most AI benefits flow to people who already have access. I want to work on products that extend those benefits to the billions who do not, whether through EdTech, healthcare, or financial tools designed for underserved markets. SeedlingLabs has shown me what that mission looks like in practice.
02
Building at the Technical-Business Interface
The most valuable roles in the next decade will belong to people who can translate between engineering teams and business strategy, who understand why a model works and can articulate why the market cares. That is the seat I am training for, and why IBE is the right program for me.

Founding Something of My Own
Entrepreneurship is not a long-term aspiration. It is the thread that runs through everything I do: founding a debate club, building products from scratch, competing in business accelerators. At some point, those skills converge into a company. I am accumulating experience intentionally toward that moment.

Research With Real-World Application
My work in number theory and neuroscience is not academic padding. I want to understand how abstract systems work because that pattern recognition carries over into product development and investment thinking. I am drawn to research that produces mental models, not just papers.
